## Garhwali Lyrics

खुदेड़ बामरी खुदेड़ बामरी
खेतों मा काम लागी
ओ बामरी खुदेड़ बामरी

हाथों मा दराती लेई
पहाड़ों की राह चली
सुबह से सांझ तक
खेतों मा काम करी

खुदेड़ बामरी खुदेड़ बामरी
धरती मा बीज बोई
अन्न उगाया मेहनत से
पहाड़ों की माटी सोई

## English Translation

Dig the earth, O Bamari, dig the earth,
Work has begun in the fields,
O Bamari, dig the earth.

With sickle in hand,
She walks the mountain paths,
From dawn till dusk,
Working the fields.

Dig the earth, O Bamari, dig the earth,
Sow the seeds in the soil,
Growing grain through hard labor,
The mountain earth has awakened.

## Cultural Context

Agricultural work-songs form one of the largest categories of Garhwali folk music. Women, who bear the primary responsibility of terrace farming in the hills, developed these rhythmic songs to coordinate group labor and make the grueling work more bearable.

"Khuded" refers to the act of digging or hoeing the terraced fields (khet) that define the Garhwali landscape. "Bamari" is an affectionate term of address for a working companion. These songs follow the agricultural calendar closely — different songs for plowing, sowing, weeding, and harvesting.

The terraced farming system of Garhwal is an engineering marvel developed over centuries, carving flat cultivable land from steep mountain slopes. The songs that accompany this work carry within them generations of agricultural knowledge and the resilience of hill women.
