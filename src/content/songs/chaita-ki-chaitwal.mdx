## Garhwali Lyrics

चैता की चैत्वाल आई गे
फूल फुलारी सजी गे

ओ रंगीलो चैत महीनो
पहाड़ों मा बसंत आई गे
चैता की चैत्वाल आई गे

घुघुती बोली डाळी मा
काफल पाकी गे माळी मा
रंग बिरंगा फूल खिलेन
खुसी छा गे सारी घाटी मा

चैता की चैत्वाल आई गे
फूल फुलारी सजी गे

## English Translation

The spring song of Chaitra has arrived,
The flowers and garlands are adorned.

O colorful month of Chaitra,
Spring has come to the mountains.
The spring song of Chaitra has arrived.

The cuckoo calls from the branches,
The kafal has ripened on the hillside,
Colorful flowers have bloomed,
Joy has spread across the entire valley.

The spring song of Chaitra has arrived,
The flowers and garlands are adorned.

## Cultural Context

Chaitwal songs are a distinct genre in Garhwali folk music, sung exclusively during the month of Chaitra (March–April) when the hills come alive with rhododendron, kafal berries, and wildflowers. These songs mark the transition from the cold winter months to the celebratory spring season.

Women traditionally sing Chaitwal songs while working in the fields, collecting forest produce, or during community gatherings at village chowks. The songs reference the cuckoo (ghughuti), which holds special significance in Pahadi culture — its call announces the arrival of spring and is believed to remind people of absent loved ones.

The Chaitra month also coincides with many fairs (melas) across Garhwal, and these songs form the musical backdrop of the season's celebrations.
