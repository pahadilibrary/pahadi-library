## Garhwali Lyrics

मंगल गीत गाओ री सखियों
आज मंगल का दिन आयो
ओ सखियों मंगल गीत गाओ

हल्दी लगाओ गोरी को
फूलों से सजाओ आंगन को
माँ के आंसू छलके
बिटिया की विदाई का दिन आयो

मंगल गीत गाओ री सखियों
ढोलक की थाप मा
नाचो गाओ खुसी मा
पर आंखी मा पानी आयो

## English Translation

Sing auspicious songs, O friends,
Today the blessed day has come,
O friends, sing the Mangal songs.

Apply turmeric on the bride,
Decorate the courtyard with flowers,
Mother's tears overflow,
The day of daughter's farewell has come.

Sing auspicious songs, O friends,
To the beat of the dholak,
Dance and sing in joy,
But tears fill the eyes.

## Cultural Context

Mangal Geet are the auspicious songs that form the emotional backbone of Garhwali weddings. Sung exclusively by women, they accompany every ritual from the haldi (turmeric) ceremony to the heart-wrenching vidaai (bride's departure).

In Garhwali culture, a daughter's departure from her maiti (parental home) is the most emotional moment of the wedding. The songs deliberately oscillate between joy and sorrow — celebrating the new beginning while mourning the separation. This duality is considered essential and healthy; suppressing grief is not part of Pahadi tradition.

The women gather in groups, sitting in the courtyard, singing songs that have been passed down through generations. Each song carries specific instructions about rituals (when to apply haldi, how to tie the sacred thread) woven into melodic verses. The Mangal Geet tradition ensures that ritual knowledge is preserved and transmitted through music rather than written texts.

These songs also serve as a space for women's commentary on social life — gentle humor about in-laws, advice to the bride, and affirmation of feminine bonds that sustain Pahadi society.
