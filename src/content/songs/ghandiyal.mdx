## Garhwali Lyrics

घण्डियाल बाजण लागी
बारात चली ऊंचा पहाड़ा
ओ बारात चली ऊंचा पहाड़ा

ढोल दमौ की धुन मा
नाचदा गीत गान्दा
दूल्हा राजा सजीं बारात
ऊंचा पहाड़ों मा जान्दा

घण्डियाल बाजण लागी
खुसी की घड़ी आई
सारा गौं मा रौनक छायी
बारात सजी के जाई

## English Translation

The wedding bells have begun to ring,
The procession moves through the high mountains,
O the procession moves through the high mountains.

To the rhythm of dhol and damau,
Dancing and singing songs,
The groom's decorated procession
Winds through the high peaks.

The wedding bells have begun to ring,
The hour of joy has arrived,
The whole village shines with celebration,
The adorned procession moves forth.

## Cultural Context

Garhwali weddings are multi-day affairs where music plays a central role. The ghandiyal (bells) are an essential part of the baraat (groom's procession), which often involves hours-long treks through mountain trails to reach the bride's village.

The dhol (large drum) and damau (small kettledrum) are the signature instruments of Garhwali ceremonial music, played by hereditary musicians called "Auji" or "Bajgi." These musicians hold a revered position in village life, as no auspicious occasion can begin without their rhythms.

Wedding processions in the hills are distinctly different from those in the plains — the narrow mountain paths, the sounds echoing through valleys, and the entire village joining in song create an atmosphere unique to Pahadi culture. The groom traditionally arrives on horseback or on foot, accompanied by the entire village.
