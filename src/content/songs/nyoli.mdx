## Garhwali Lyrics

ओ होई, ऊंचा पहाड़ों मा
मेरा मन रोई जान्दा
दूर देसा मा बसी गई
न्योली गीत गाई जान्दा

ओ होई, जब सें तू गई
पहाड़ सुनसान है गया
खेतों की पगडंडी मा
तेरी याद सतान्दा

ओ होई, ऊंचा पहाड़ों मा
मेरा मन रोई जान्दा

## English Translation

O hoi, in these high mountains,
My heart weeps endlessly,
Gone to live in a far-off land,
I keep singing the Nyoli song.

O hoi, since you left,
The mountains have gone silent,
On the footpaths between the fields,
Your memory haunts me still.

O hoi, in these high mountains,
My heart weeps endlessly.

## Cultural Context

Nyoli is perhaps the most emotionally potent genre in all of Garhwali folk music. It is the song of viraha — the ache of separation from a loved one, usually a wife separated from her husband who has migrated for work, or a woman longing for her maiti (parental home) after marriage.

The distinctive "O hoi" call that begins each Nyoli is designed to carry across mountain valleys. Traditionally, women would sing Nyoli from hilltops or while working in terraced fields, the melody echoing from ridge to ridge. It was — and in some remote areas still is — a form of long-distance emotional communication.

Migration has been a defining feature of Garhwali life for centuries. Men travel to the plains for employment (a pattern called "palaayan"), leaving women to manage farms, families, and villages. Nyoli songs are the musical expression of this enduring separation, carrying within them the loneliness of mountain villages and the unshakeable bonds of love and home.

The genre has been recognized by musicologists as unique to the Garhwal Himalayas, with no direct parallel in other Indian folk traditions.
