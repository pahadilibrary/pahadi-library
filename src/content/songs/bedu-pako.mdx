## Garhwali Lyrics

ओ नरैणी काफल पाको चैता

बेडू पाको बारा मासा
नरैणी काफल पाको चैता

हिटो ए दीदी, हिटो ए दीदी
भैणी दराती लेई हिटो
ओ हिजो के बतौन, हिजो के बतौन
सासु मैत बिटीन कें हिटो

नरैणी काफल पाको चैता

## English Translation

The bedu fruit ripens through all twelve months,
O Naraini, the kafal ripens only in Chaitra.

Walk along, O sister, walk along,
Take your sickle, sister, and walk along.
As they said yesterday, as was told,
Mother-in-law sent her daughter to her maternal home.

The bedu fruit ripens through all twelve months,
O Naraini, the kafal ripens only in Chaitra.

## Cultural Context

"Bedu Pako Bara Masa" is widely considered the unofficial anthem of Uttarakhand. The song celebrates the bedu (wild fig) tree, which bears fruit throughout the year, contrasted with the kafal berry that ripens only in Chaitra (March–April). The song was popularized by the legendary Garhwali singer Narendra Singh Negi and has become a symbol of Pahadi cultural identity.

The imagery of the sickle-carrying sister walking to the fields reflects the central role of women in Garhwali agrarian life. The mention of a daughter visiting her maternal home (maiti) touches on one of the deepest emotional currents in Pahadi culture — the bond between a married woman and her parental home.

This song is sung at festivals, gatherings, cultural programs, and has been covered by countless artists. It transcends regional boundaries within Uttarakhand, beloved equally in Kumaon and Garhwal.
