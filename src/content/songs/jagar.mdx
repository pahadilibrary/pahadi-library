## Garhwali Lyrics

जागो जागो देवता जागो
आज तुमारी पूजा हुई
ओ देवता जागो

धूप दीप की ज्योति मा
घंटी की आवाज मा
जागरी की वाणी सुणी
देवता जागी जान्दा

जागो जागो देवता जागो
पहाड़ों के रखवाला
गौं का दुख हरो
सब का भला करो

## English Translation

Awaken, awaken, O deity, awaken,
Today your worship is offered,
O deity, awaken.

In the glow of incense and lamp,
In the sound of the ritual bell,
Hearing the Jagari's sacred voice,
The deity begins to stir.

Awaken, awaken, O deity, awaken,
Guardian of the mountains,
Remove the village's sorrows,
Bring welfare to all.

## Cultural Context

The Jagar is one of the most ancient and powerful ritual traditions of Garhwal. It is an all-night ceremony in which a Jagari (the ritual singer-narrator) recites epic ballads to invoke local deities and ancestral spirits. The deity is believed to manifest through a medium called the "Dangariya" who enters a trance-like state.

The Jagari holds an extraordinarily important role in Garhwali society — they are the keepers of oral history, genealogy, and mythological knowledge. Through hours-long musical narratives, they recount the deeds of local deities (devtas) like Nanda Devi, Bhairav, and the various Narsingh manifestations unique to each valley.

Accompanied by the dhol, damau, and thali (plate used as a percussion instrument), the Jagar creates a hypnotic musical atmosphere. The tradition has been recognized as an Intangible Cultural Heritage and represents one of the oldest continuous oral traditions in the Himalayan region.

Each village in Garhwal has its own local deity (gram devta), and the Jagar ceremonies maintain the living relationship between communities and their divine protectors. The practice is threatened by migration and modernization, making documentation efforts critically important.
