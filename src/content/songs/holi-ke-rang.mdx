## Garhwali Lyrics

होली के रंग बरसे
गौं गौं मा खुसी छाई
ढोल दमौ बाजदा
होली खेलण सब आई

रंगों की बौछार मा
सब रंगी गे एक रंग मा
ऊंचा नीचा सब भूली गे
होली के मस्त मलंग मा

होली के रंग बरसे
पहाड़ों मा बसंत आई
गौं की गलियों मा
खुसी की धारा बही जाई

## English Translation

The colors of Holi rain down,
Joy has spread through every village,
Dhol and damau are playing,
Everyone has come to celebrate Holi.

In the shower of colors,
All are painted in one hue,
High and low, all distinctions forgotten,
In the joyous abandon of Holi.

The colors of Holi rain down,
Spring has come to the mountains,
Through the village lanes,
A river of happiness flows on.

## Cultural Context

Garhwali Holi is celebrated distinctively in three forms: Baithki Holi (seated musical gatherings), Khadi Holi (standing/dancing Holi), and Mahila Holi (women's Holi). The Baithki Holi tradition involves community members gathering in the evenings for weeks before the main festival, singing classical and semi-classical compositions adapted to the Garhwali idiom.

This tradition is particularly strong in towns like Gopeshwar, Nandprayag, and the villages of Chamoli and Rudraprayag districts. The singing sessions begin from Vasant Panchami (late January/early February) and continue until the day of Holi.

The dhol-damau accompaniment gives Garhwali Holi songs their distinctive rhythm, different from the Holi songs of the plains. The emphasis on community gathering and collective singing — rather than just color-throwing — reflects the deeper social fabric of Pahadi culture where music is the primary medium of communal joy.
