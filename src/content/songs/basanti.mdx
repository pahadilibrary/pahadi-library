## Garhwali Lyrics

बसन्ती हवा चली आई
बुरांश फूली गे पहाड़ों मा
ओ बसन्ती हवा चली आई

लाल लाल फूलों से सजी
पहाड़ों की हर डाळी
सर्दी गई बसंत आई
खुसी मा झूमी जान्दी घाटी

बसन्ती हवा चली आई
काफल पाकी गे डाळी मा
घुघुती बोली ए बसंत मा
याद आई अपणी माटी मा

## English Translation

The spring breeze has arrived,
The rhododendrons bloom on the mountains,
O the spring breeze has arrived.

Adorned with crimson flowers,
Every branch of the mountains,
Winter has gone, spring has come,
The valley sways in joy.

The spring breeze has arrived,
The kafal has ripened on the branch,
The cuckoo sings in this spring,
Memories stir of my homeland.

घुघुती इस बसंत में बोल रही है,
याद आई अपनी माटी की।

## Cultural Context

Basanti songs herald the arrival of spring in the Garhwal hills, when the hillsides transform with the fiery blooms of the buransh (Rhododendron arboreum) — the state tree of Uttarakhand. The buransh is not merely decorative; its flowers are used to make a traditional juice (buransh ka sharbat) believed to have medicinal properties for the heart.

The kafal (Myrica esculenta), a tart wild berry that ripens in spring, is central to Pahadi childhood memories and appears in countless folk songs. There is even a folk tale about a mother who asked her daughter to guard the kafal harvest; the daughter died in the sun, and her spirit became the cuckoo (ghughuti) that calls "kafal pako, meen nee chakho" — "the kafal is ripe, I haven't tasted it."

Spring in the Garhwal hills is a season of renewal, migration of birds, and the beginning of a new agricultural cycle. Basanti songs capture this moment of transformation, mixing sensory imagery (red flowers, sweet berries, birdsong) with the ever-present emotion of nostalgia that defines the Pahadi soul.
