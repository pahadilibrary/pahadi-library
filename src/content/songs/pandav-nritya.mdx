## Garhwali Lyrics

पाण्डव बीरा जागो
धर्म की राह चलो
ओ पाण्डव बीरा जागो

कौरवों से लड़ाई छ
धर्म अधर्म की लड़ाई छ
द्रौपदी की पुकार सुणी
पाण्डव बीरा उठो जागो

अर्जुन का बाण चले
भीम की गदा गरजे
धर्म की जीत होली
अधर्म का नाश होलो

## English Translation

Awaken, O brave Pandavas,
Walk the path of righteousness,
O brave Pandavas, awaken.

The battle with the Kauravas is upon us,
The war between right and wrong,
Hear Draupadi's cry,
Rise, O brave Pandavas, awaken.

Arjuna's arrows fly,
Bhima's mace thunders,
Righteousness shall triumph,
Evil shall be destroyed.

## Cultural Context

The Pandav Lila (also called Pandav Nritya) is a spectacular ritualistic dance-drama unique to Garhwal that retells the entire Mahabharata over a period of days or even weeks. It is considered the longest folk performance in the world and has been documented by UNESCO.

Performed in the villages of Chamoli, Rudraprayag, and parts of Tehri district, the Pandav Lila is not mere entertainment but a sacred ritual. Village men take on the roles of the five Pandavas, Draupadi, and Krishna, and during the performance, they are believed to be genuinely possessed by these characters. The entire village participates as both audience and supporting cast.

The songs that accompany Pandav Nritya narrate the epic in Garhwali, adapting the Sanskrit Mahabharata to local landscape and culture. Mountains become the settings for battles, local rivers replace mythological ones, and the Pandavas are portrayed as Pahadi warriors familiar with the terrain.

Garhwali folk tradition holds a deep connection to the Mahabharata — many villages trace their ancestry to the Pandavas, and place names across the region (Joshimath from "Jyotirmath," Pandukeshwar) reflect this living mythology.
